Bioplastics 

Creating bioplastics using agar, glycerin, and gelatin involves harnessing natural polymers to craft a flexible and biodegradable material. Agar, a seaweed-derived substance, forms the base of the bioplastic, lending it structural integrity. Glycerin enhances flexibility, ensuring the bioplastic can adapt to various applications. Gelatin serves to strengthen the material, contributing to its durability and performance. Together, these ingredients offer a sustainable alternative to traditional plastics, promoting environmental conservation without compromising functionality.
